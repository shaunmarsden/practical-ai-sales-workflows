# Workflow 02: Post Call Follow Up

## The Problem

After a sales call, the notes, actions, email and CRM update are often completed at different times. Important context gets lost and a confident sounding first draft can quietly invent momentum that was never agreed.

## The Job of the Workflow

Turn a call transcript or clear set of notes into:

1. A short call summary
2. Confirmed facts and estimates
3. Agreed actions with owners and dates
4. Missing information and points to check
5. A natural follow up email draft
6. Suggested CRM updates

The workflow prepares these items. It does not send the email or update the CRM.

## Inputs

Use only permitted information:

- Call transcript or notes
- Current CRM record when relevant
- Relevant prior email thread
- Approved public information when needed

## Process

### 1. Extract the Evidence

Identify what was directly stated, what was estimated, what was discussed without agreement and what is still unknown.

### 2. Check the Commitments

For every action, record:

- Owner
- Exact commitment
- Date or timing
- Evidence from the call

Do not turn a suggestion into an agreement.

### 3. Draft the Follow Up

Write a short email that sounds like the salesperson. Include only agreed actions and use placeholders where a time or detail still needs checking.

### 4. Suggest CRM Updates

Prepare CRM notes and fields for review. Clearly label them as suggestions and do not claim they have been saved.

### 5. Run the Human Check

Before using anything, confirm:

- Every important statement is supported
- Estimates are labelled
- Dates and commitments are exact
- No meeting has been invented
- No sensitive or irrelevant details are included
- The email sounds natural
- CRM changes are still awaiting approval

## What to Measure

- Time taken from transcript to checked output
- Number of factual corrections
- Number of invented or overstated claims
- Whether every action has the right owner and timing
- How much of the email and CRM draft a person keeps
