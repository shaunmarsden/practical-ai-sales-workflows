# Fictional Example: Stalling at Signature

> This scenario is entirely fictional, created to test and calibrate this skill. Apex Logistics, Sarah, David and every detail are invented. Do not use real customer data here.

## Context

- **Account:** Apex Logistics (Fictional)
- **Stage:** Negotiation / Pending Signature
- **Stakeholder:** Sarah (VP of Operations, Champion), David (CFO, Economic Buyer)

## Evidence (Excerpt from Email Thread)

**Sarah (VP Ops):** "Hi team, David is reviewing the final MSA. He's happy with the pricing, but he just asked if we could do a 3-month pilot before signing the full 2-year agreement to 'make sure the integration works as smoothly as promised.' Let me know what you think."

## Expected AI Diagnosis

- **Primary Driver:** Late-stage indecision / risk mitigation by the economic buyer (David).
- **Evidence:** "make sure the integration works as smoothly as promised" indicates fear of implementation failure, not a pricing objection.
- **Not an Objection:** David is not arguing the price is too high; he is trying to de-risk the deployment.
- **Missing Information:** We do not know if a 3-month pilot is commercially viable for us, nor do we have evidence of what specific integration risks David is worried about.
