# Fictional Example: Stalling at Signature

> This scenario is entirely fictional, created to test and calibrate this skill. Apex Logistics, Sarah, David and every detail are invented. Do not use real customer data here.

## Context

- **Account:** Apex Logistics (Fictional)
- **Stage:** Negotiation / Pending Signature
- **Stakeholder:** Sarah (VP of Operations, Champion), David (CFO, Economic Buyer)

## Evidence (Excerpt from Email Thread)

**Sarah (VP Ops):** "Hi team, David is reviewing the final MSA. He's happy with the pricing, but he just asked if we could do a 3-month pilot before signing the full 2-year agreement to 'make sure the integration works as smoothly as promised.' Let me know what you think."

## Expected AI Diagnosis

- **Primary Driver:** Late-stage indecision, risk mitigation by the economic buyer (David), though this is a genuinely debatable call, not a clean one.
- **Evidence:** "make sure the integration works as smoothly as promised" indicates fear of implementation failure, tied to committing to the full two-year term, rather than a pricing objection. David has not disputed the value or the price.
- **Why This Is Deliberately Ambiguous:** David's ask is specific and answerable, a three-month pilot, which on the surface reads like a standard information or risk objection: something you answer and move past. What points toward indecision instead is the size of the commitment he is avoiding. He is not questioning whether the integration works in principle; he is avoiding a two-year commitment without more proof, which is decision risk wearing an objection's clothing. A confident label of either reading is defensible. The skill should flag the ambiguity rather than pick one with unwarranted certainty.
- **Missing Information:** We do not know if a three-month pilot is commercially viable, nor do we have evidence of what specific integration risks David is worried about.

## What a Weak Diagnosis Would Do

- Confidently label this a standard objection and treat David's ask as a simple, closed question to answer, missing that the underlying driver is reluctance to commit to the full term.
- Confidently label this indecision and ignore that David has given a specific, answerable request, which deserves a real answer regardless of the underlying driver.
- Recommend a discount, a guarantee, or an unauthorised pilot extension to resolve either reading.
