# Review a Lost Opportunity

> Landed here directly rather than clicking through from a guide? This file is the instruction sheet an AI assistant follows, not written for a first read start to finish. [What is a sales AI skill?](../../../guides/what-is-a-sales-ai-skill.md) has the plain-English version.

A stated reason for a loss is not always the whole story, and the person who said no is not always the whole opportunity. This skill exists to tell the difference between a deal that is genuinely dead and one that is only blocked, before either chasing something that has clearly ended or writing off something that has not.

## Gather the Inputs

- The final message, stated reason, or CRM record marking the loss
- Anything known about what changed on the other side: a stakeholder leaving, a reorganisation, a budget cycle, a competitor decision
- The evidence originally built for this opportunity, such as a business case, a pilot result, or a proof point, so it can be checked for reuse

## Separate Fact from Assumption

Start with exactly what was said or shown, not the story that would make the most sense of it. A stated reason often blends more than one factor together, budget, timing, a stakeholder change, a lingering objection. Do not collapse it into a single tidy explanation the evidence does not actually support.

## Check Whether the Underlying Problem Still Exists

A stakeholder leaving, a reorganisation, or one objection landing badly does not automatically mean the business problem this deal was solving has gone away. Check whether the evidence for that problem, a pilot result, a measured saving, a real pain point, still stands on its own, independent of who raised it or who is still involved.

## Classify the Loss

Work out which of these is the closest fit, and say so honestly rather than defaulting to whichever is more comfortable:

- `disqualification`: the fit was never really there, or a hard blocker such as budget, compliance, or authority is real and unlikely to change
- `timing`: the fit is real, but now is not the right moment. A stated cycle, a reorganisation, or a stated future point usually means exactly that, not a polite no
- `stakeholder-change`: the specific person the deal ran through is no longer the route in, but the underlying evidence and problem may still be usable through someone else
- `unresolved-objection`: something specific was raised, an answer or a workaround was actually sent back, and it was rejected, or a genuine back-and-forth happened without landing
- `no-decision`: something specific was raised, an answer or a workaround was sent back, and then contact from both sides simply stops. Nobody said no. This is not the prospect quietly deciding against it; it is a live thread nobody returned to. Do not fold this into `unresolved-objection` as if the prospect were unsatisfied; the difference matters because the next action is not waiting for an external trigger, it is following up on the answer that was already sent

## Decide What Would Justify Coming Back

Do not default to either extreme: silently writing it off forever, or scheduling another chase straight away. If there is a real trigger stated by the prospect, a named future cycle, a new stakeholder in the role, name it specifically. If there genuinely is not one, say that plainly instead of inventing a hopeful plan to soften the loss.

## Apply the Guardrails

- Never invent the reason for a loss beyond what was actually said or shown.
- Never invent a specific future date or trigger where only a vague one was given; treat vague as vague.
- Never assume an earlier, separate objection was the deciding factor without evidence that it was.
- Never treat an internal, company-specific CRM stage label as evidence of why or how a deal was lost unless something in the record actually defines it. A stage name that reads as self-explanatory may not be; confirm what it means before using it to support a classification, or say plainly that it is unconfirmed.
- Keep any reusable evidence, a pilot result, a proof point, flagged as reusable, since it does not expire just because this particular opportunity did not close.

## Stop When the Task Is Unsafe

Do not produce a confident classification when:

- The stated reason is too thin or too vague to distinguish disqualification from timing
- Conflicting evidence exists about why the deal was lost, and nothing resolves the conflict
- The request is to manufacture a reason to re-approach a prospect who has given a clear, unconditional no

Say so and name exactly what evidence would resolve the uncertainty, rather than picking a classification to seem decisive.

## Require Human Review

This produces an analysis and a recommendation. Updating the CRM stage, setting a follow-up reminder, and any actual re-approach stay subject to explicit human decision.
