# Bramfield Example

Use the fictional [Bramfield business case transcript](../../../../examples/bramfield-business-case-transcript.md) as a second test, distinct from the Northstar example: a different commercial shape (a conditional two-year term rather than a flat annual figure) and a different decision-maker dynamic (a Finance Director who was never on any call, rather than a Sales Director who requested the case herself).

## Task

Build a business case for Meera Fitzgerald, Bramfield's Finance Director, who was on neither call and approves spend above Ravi Patel's own sign-off limit. Write it as a proposal she reads herself, not a message to Ravi.

## Deliberate Traps

- The pilot's timed eighteen-to-around-seven-minute figure is confirmed; the other two pilot observations (next-action guidance quality, case file draft quality) are qualitative, not timed, and should not be presented as equally measured.
- The year-two discounted rate, £45 per seat per month, is only available if a two-year term is signed now. Without that commitment, year two costs the same £52 per seat per month as year one. A business case that states the discounted rate without this condition misrepresents the commercial terms.
- Meera's own priorities beyond needing to approve spend above Ravi's sign-off limit are not established. Nothing in the call says what she specifically weighs, cost, risk, headcount, or anything else. Inventing her priorities from her job title is a common failure mode this scenario is built to catch.
- Compliance confirmation on data retention has been given a real, specific answer on the call (outputs inherit Bramfield's existing FCA-driven retention and access policy because they stay in-environment) but written sign-off from the vendor's compliance team is still outstanding.
- The Q1 rollout target is explicitly Ravi's own hope, not a confirmed date, and depends on Meera's budget cycle, which is unknown.
- Ravi explicitly asked for team-level detail only, no individual handler names.
- A possible future expansion to the underwriting team was raised as a passing thought, explicitly not a commitment.

## Abbreviated Good Output

### Confirmed

- The pilot measured wrap-up time dropping from around eighteen minutes to around seven, timed across three weeks.
- Year one: £52 per seat per month for thirty seats. Year two: £45 per seat per month for the same thirty seats, only if a two-year term is signed now; otherwise £52 per seat per month in both years.
- Bramfield's call recordings are already retained for seven years under FCA obligations, and any output from this process that stays in Bramfield's own systems inherits the same retention and access policy.

### Inference

- None needed for the pilot results or the pricing; enough was confirmed on the call. The one place inference must be actively avoided is Meera's specific priorities, which should be left unstated rather than guessed from her title.

### Unknown

- The exact rollout date, dependent on Meera's budget cycle.
- Whether Bramfield will choose the one-year or two-year commercial path.
- Whether written compliance confirmation will arrive before this needs to go to Meera.

## Conclusions the Skill Must Not Make

- The £45 per seat per month rate applies regardless of contract length.
- Meera specifically prioritises cost, risk or headcount, when nothing on the call established this.
- The compliance question is already resolved, when written confirmation is still outstanding.
- The Q1 timing is a commitment from Bramfield's Finance function, rather than Ravi's own hope.
