# Cedarwell Example

A fictional cold-outbound scenario, separate from the Northstar Analytics story, since outbound prospecting happens before any call exists.

> This scenario is entirely fictional. Cedarwell Group, its people, and its job advert were created to test this skill.

## Task

Research shows Cedarwell Group, a 60-person regional logistics company, has an open job advert for an "Operations Reporting Coordinator" that mentions "manually compiling weekly delivery and exception reports across three depot systems" as a core responsibility. Cedarwell's Operations Director, Marcus Webb, is named in a recent local business press piece about the company's depot expansion.

Select the target, choose the angle, and draft the first-touch message.

## Deliberate Traps

- The job advert is a real, specific, verifiable signal (manual cross-system reporting). A generic "logistics companies are adopting AI" observation would not be grounded in anything Cedarwell-specific and should not be used as the hook instead.
- Marcus Webb's role (Operations Director) makes him a plausible buyer for an operations problem; the reporting coordinator role itself is not the buyer.
- The depot expansion is public information and can be mentioned as context, but is not itself proof that Cedarwell has a reporting problem. The job advert is the actual evidence; the expansion only explains why the company might be growing pains-prone.
- Cedarwell's size (60 people) and named contact route make it a reasonably good-fit target. Nothing here should inflate that into an urgent, must-act-now framing that the evidence does not support.

## Abbreviated Good Output

### Target scoring

Score up: named reachable buyer (Marcus Webb, Operations Director), a specific verifiable signal (the job advert's own wording), plausible authority over an operations tooling or process decision.

Score down: nothing significant here; this is a reasonably strong target.

### First-touch draft

```text
Subject: Manual reporting across three depot systems

Hi Marcus,

Compiling weekly delivery and exception reports across three separate depot systems by hand is exactly the kind of recurring work that tends to eat a disproportionate amount of a coordinator's week, and it's the sort of gap that shows up in job ads before anyone frames it as a project.

I'd be glad to put together a short first look at where that reporting workflow could realistically be simplified, and roughly what that could be worth in coordinator time back, based on how the process is currently split across systems.

If useful, reply and I'll get a first cut over.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Conclusions the Skill Must Not Make

- Cedarwell has an urgent, immediate problem requiring immediate action; the evidence supports a real signal, not urgency.
- The depot expansion itself proves a reporting problem; the job advert is the actual evidence, the expansion is only context.
- A meeting should be the first ask; the low-friction reply-based offer is the correct first touch here, not a calendar link.
- Marcus Webb has already expressed interest; nothing here indicates a reply yet.
