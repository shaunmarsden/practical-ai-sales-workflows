# Follow-Up Email Template and Checklist

## Template shape

Define your own fixed wording once, then treat it as locked. The value of a fixed template is that it removes restructuring as a decision available to the drafting step; only the marked fields change.

```
Hi [First name],

[Opening line: one specific detail from the call, not a generic restatement.]

[One sentence naming what the attached resource covers for this recipient specifically.]

Resources:
- [Resource one, live link on descriptive text]
- [Resource two, live link on descriptive text]

[One clear next step.]

[Any relevant date or availability detail, ordinal suffix.]

Best,
[Your name]
```

Lock the section order. Personalise the bracketed fields only.

## Template shape: recipient forwarding to someone else

When a single recipient needs to forward material to a decision maker who was not on the call and is not being sent this email, add exactly one locked block after the resources section. It does not replace or reorder anything defined above.

```
Hi [First name],

[Opening line: one specific detail from the call.]

[One sentence naming what the attached resource covers for this recipient specifically.]

Resources:
- [Resource one, live link on descriptive text]

For [the decision maker's role or name, if known]:
- [A resource built specifically for that person, live link on descriptive text]

[Only if genuinely true: an offer to speak with that person directly, so the recipient is not left making the case alone.]

[One clear next step.]

[Any relevant date or availability detail, ordinal suffix.]

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Pre-send checklist

- Does it follow the template structure exactly, with nothing reordered, merged or rewritten wholesale?
- Is every recipient addressed in the second person, with no one described in the third person anywhere in the email?
- If there is more than one recipient, is everyone included as part of "you", with nobody singled out as the sole reader?
- If a decision maker not on the call needs material to forward, is their block clearly separate and addressed in the third person, never as if they are reading the email themselves?
- Is the opening line specific to this actual call, not a generic restatement?
- Are all links live, correctly labelled, and pointing at the right destination?
- Ordinal dates, no em dashes, no filler phrases?

## Fictional worked example

Using the [Hartwell Analytics transcript](../../../../examples/hartwell-post-call-transcript.md): a generic opening line would be "It was great speaking about your sales process." A personalised one, grounded in what Alex Morgan actually said, would open with the Monday pipeline meeting problem: confirming visibility into what happened on a call is the specific thing worth naming, not a restatement of "sales process" in general.

For the forwarding case: if Alex needed a resource specifically to hand to Priya Chen ahead of budget sign-off, that block would be addressed as "For Priya, as Sales Director" and describe what it covers for her role, not repeat what was already said to Alex. It would not greet Priya directly or write as if she were reading the email, since she is not the recipient.
