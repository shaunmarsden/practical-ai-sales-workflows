# Chase Sequence Stages in Detail

Timings below are a starting point. Adjust them to match your own sales cycle; what matters is the reasoning at each stage, not the exact day count.

## Early chase (a few days after the last message)

Purpose: confirm the last resource landed and nudge toward the next concrete step.

- Open with a direct reference to something specific from the call, not the fact that a message was already sent
- One sentence on the resource or next step
- One sentence on any real upcoming date, if relevant
- One clear next step, kept short

## Middle chase (roughly a week later)

Purpose: surface a blocker or question before it becomes an unstated reason to stall.

- Open with a direct question or a reference to a specific concern raised on the call
- Offer something useful: an answer to a likely question, or a detail they might need for an internal conversation
- Leave the door open without sounding needy

## Late chase (roughly a week after that)

Purpose: re-anchor to the original problem, not the proposed solution.

- Go back to the specific pain point from the call
- If timing has genuinely moved, offer a real alternative, not a vague placeholder
- Frame around their situation, not your pipeline

## Final chase

Purpose: create genuine urgency around a real constraint.

- State a specific date or a real limit, never "end of the week" or another vague marker
- No guilt, no excessive warmth. Measured and direct.
- One clear action: reply, book a call, or confirm.

## Close-out, if there is still no reply

Purpose: acknowledge the unanswered sequence directly and close it cleanly.

- Acknowledge plainly that several messages have gone unanswered; do not write as though this is the first one
- State the practical consequence as fact, not a threat
- Leave the door open in one short, low-pressure sentence
- Make clear that the next move belongs entirely to the prospect

## What a strong anchor looks like

Weak: "Just checking if you'd had a chance to review what I sent."
Strong, using the [Northstar Analytics scenario](../../../../examples/northstar-post-call-transcript.md): "You mentioned the team spends fifteen to thirty minutes after every call on notes and CRM updates. That estimate is exactly what a first test would help you measure properly."

The anchor should be specific enough that it could not be dropped unchanged into a different prospect's message. If it could, go back to the call and find something more specific.
