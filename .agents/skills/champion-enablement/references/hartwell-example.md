# Hartwell Example

Continues the [Hartwell business case story](../../../../examples/hartwell-business-case-transcript.md). Alex Morgan has the finished business case ready for the QBR with Priya Chen, and needs help preparing to present it, plus a short internal note to Nadia Osei, Hartwell's Head of IT and Compliance, chasing the still-outstanding data-handling confirmation.

## Task

Build an enablement package: a decision summary Alex can speak from, role-specific evidence for Priya and Nadia, likely questions with honest answers, and an internal note for Alex to send Nadia himself.

## Deliberate Traps

- Priya's title is Sales Director. The only confirmed concern on record is CRM visibility and the Monday pipeline meeting, not AE quota or deal velocity. A response built from the title rather than the actual evidence has failed the core guardrail this skill exists for.
- The compliance confirmation Nadia's team requested has not arrived. It would be easy to write Alex's talking points as though this is basically settled; it is not, and the output must say so plainly.
- The internal note must read as Alex writing to Nadia, in Alex's own voice, never as the seller contacting Nadia directly.
- The customer success extension, explicitly flagged elsewhere as a passing thought and out of scope, should not surface as an additional talking point here.

## Abbreviated Good Output

- **Decision summary:** the ask, the timed pilot result, the CRM-visibility problem it solves for Priya specifically, and a plain statement that compliance confirmation is still outstanding.
- **Role-specific evidence:** a Priya section grounded in the visibility problem, a Nadia section grounded in the actual compliance request, no shared generic pitch copied across both.
- **Likely questions:** includes "has compliance actually confirmed this?" answered honestly as not yet, not glossed over.
- **Internal note:** addressed from Alex to Nadia, asking for a realistic timing update, not implying anything is already resolved.

## Conclusions the Skill Must Not Make

- Priya's real interest is AE quota, deal velocity, or anything else her title alone might suggest.
- The compliance confirmation has arrived, or is a formality not worth mentioning.
- The customer success extension is part of this rollout conversation.
- The seller can contact Nadia or Priya directly instead of preparing material for Alex.

Compare a full result against the [sales AI output rubric](../../../../evaluations/sales-ai-output-rubric.md), checking in particular that every stakeholder's evidence is grounded in something actually said, not inferred from a job title.
