# Champion Enablement

> Landed here directly rather than clicking through from a guide? This file is the instruction sheet an AI assistant follows, not written for a first read start to finish. [What is a sales AI skill?](../../../guides/what-is-a-sales-ai-skill.md) has the plain-English version.

A champion carrying a case to other stakeholders is doing work the seller cannot do directly: standing in a room, or sending an internal email, on the seller's behalf. This skill prepares what the champion needs to do that well. It does not replace the champion, contact anyone else, or assume what a stakeholder cares about just because of their job title.

## Gather the Inputs

- The evidence already established: the call record, the business case if one exists, confirmed figures and outstanding items
- Who the champion is actually presenting to or forwarding this to, and in what setting, a meeting, an internal email, a forwarded document
- Anything already known about each further stakeholder's actual concerns, from something they or the champion have said, not guessed from their title
- What is still outstanding or unconfirmed, so it is not accidentally presented as settled

## Map Stakeholders to Evidence, Not to Titles

For each further stakeholder, record only what is actually known about what they care about. A title suggests an area of responsibility, not a confirmed priority: a Sales Director's title does not by itself confirm she cares about headcount efficiency rather than pipeline visibility, and an IT lead's title does not by itself confirm exactly what their compliance concern is beyond what has already been stated. Where nothing is actually known about a stakeholder's concern beyond their role, say so, rather than inventing a plausible-sounding priority to fill the gap.

## Build the Enablement Package

1. **A decision summary for the champion to speak from**, not a document to hand over unread: the ask, the evidence behind it, and the one or two points most likely to matter to the audience in this specific meeting.
2. **Role-specific evidence**, one short section per further stakeholder, using only what is actually known about their concern. Never repeat the same generic pitch under each stakeholder's name.
3. **Questions likely to come up, and an honest answer to each**, including "not yet confirmed" where that is genuinely the answer. An unconfirmed item stays unconfirmed here; it does not get quietly resolved to make the champion look more prepared.
4. **An internal note, if the situation calls for one**, drafted for the champion to send in their own name to a specific further stakeholder, never addressed as though the seller were sending it directly.

## Apply the Guardrails

- Never contact, or draft something addressed to, a stakeholder beyond the champion without the champion's explicit involvement. Everything produced here is for the champion to use, send or present themselves.
- Never assume a stakeholder's priority from their job title alone. If nothing is actually known, say so.
- Never resolve an outstanding item, a pending confirmation, an unconfirmed figure, into a settled fact to make the champion's case look stronger than it is.
- Never invent a question a stakeholder is likely to ask; base anticipated questions on what the evidence itself leaves open.

## Stop When the Task Is Unsafe

Do not produce an enablement package when:

- No champion has actually agreed to carry this internally; drafting material for someone who has not agreed to present it risks it being used without their real buy-in
- The request is to draft something addressed directly to a further stakeholder rather than material for the champion to use
- The underlying evidence is too thin to say anything role-specific without guessing

Explain the limitation and ask for the minimum missing detail instead.

## Require Human Review

The champion, and the seller supporting them, must check that every claim is one the champion is actually prepared to stand behind in the room, and that nothing outstanding has been upgraded to settled. Sending any internal note, and presenting the case itself, stays with the champion.
