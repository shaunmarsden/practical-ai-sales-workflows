# Output Schema

Use this structure unless the user requests a smaller subset. Keep sections concise and omit empty sections only when they are genuinely irrelevant.

## 1. Source Inventory

List every supplied source and any important limitation.

Include:

- Source type
- Date when known
- Whether it appears current
- Missing context that affects reliability

Do not imply access to a source that was not supplied or retrieved.

## 2. Call Summary

Summarise the purpose, important discussion and current position in no more than five bullets.

Use only supported information. Do not add persuasive language or assume the opportunity will progress.

## 3. Confirmed Facts

Record directly supported facts. Attribute anything material to the relevant source.

Do not include estimates, second hand comments or interpretations here.

## 4. Estimates

Record approximate numbers or views that a source explicitly described as an estimate.

Include:

- The estimate
- Who supplied it
- Whether it has been measured or verified

## 5. Problems and Desired Outcomes

Capture the problems and outcomes expressed during the call. Prefer the customer's own language.

Do not convert a salesperson's hypothesis into a customer problem.

## 6. Stakeholders

Record each named stakeholder and classify their involvement:

- Confirmed role
- Reported role
- Possible involvement
- Unknown influence

Mark comments about an absent stakeholder as `second_hand`.

## 7. Signals, Concerns and Objections

Separate:

- Confirmed buying signals
- Polite or general interest
- Stated concerns
- Possible interpretations to test

Do not describe interest as intent to buy without supporting evidence.

## 8. Actions and Commitments

Use a table with these columns:

| Owner | Action | Timing | Condition | Evidence Status | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Preserve relative timing when the call date is unknown. Keep conditions attached to commitments.

Trigger a human review flag when the owner, timing or condition is unclear.

## 9. Unknowns and Missing Qualification

List material gaps such as:

- Budget
- Authority
- Decision process
- Priority
- Timing
- Technical or data constraints
- Unconfirmed stakeholders

Do not fill gaps with typical industry assumptions.

## 10. Inferences to Test

State each interpretation as a question or testable hypothesis.

Label it `inference` and explain which evidence led to it. Never present it as confirmed customer language.

## 11. Recommended Next Action

Suggest one proportionate next action and explain why it follows from the evidence.

Do not claim that the action has been completed. Flag any diary, permission or stakeholder check required first.

## 12. CRM Ready Summary

Prepare a short neutral summary for human review.

Include:

- Current situation
- Confirmed problem
- Desired outcome
- Agreed actions
- Important unknowns

Do not claim that the CRM has been updated.

## 13. Human Review Flags

End with anything that must be checked before the output is used.

Always flag:

- Source conflicts
- Unsupported dates or commitments
- Conditional actions
- Potentially stale CRM information
- Sensitive information that may be unnecessary
- External actions still awaiting approval
