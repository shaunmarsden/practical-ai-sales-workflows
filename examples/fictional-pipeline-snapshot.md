# Fictional Pipeline Snapshot

> This is an entirely fictional pipeline export, created to test the Pipeline Evidence Review workflow. Every company, person, figure and date is invented. It reuses the [Hartwell Analytics story](hartwell-post-call-transcript.md) for one deal and adds four more. Nothing here is a real CRM record.

The salesperson is Shaun, a fictional account executive. Below is a snapshot of his open pipeline as it currently reads in the CRM, followed by the notes and evidence actually available for each deal. The point of the scenario is that the CRM fields and the evidence do not always agree.

Today's date for this snapshot is 15 October.

## What the CRM Says

| Deal | Stage | Value | Close date | Last activity | Next step in CRM |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| Hartwell Analytics | Negotiation | £48,000 | 30 September | 12 September | (blank) |
| Cedarwell Group | Qualification | £30,000 | 31 October | 9 October | Send case study |
| Meridian Freight | Proposal Sent | £72,000 | 30 September | 2 September | Chase proposal |
| Harbourview Media | Verbal Commit | £55,000 | 20 October | 25 September | Send contract |
| Oakline Dental Group | Discovery | £18,000 | 30 November | 11 October | Second discovery call, booked 18 October |

## What Is Actually Known, Per Deal

### Hartwell Analytics

- The pilot succeeded, with a measured drop from around twenty-five minutes to under ten minutes of post-call admin per account executive (see the [business case output](hartwell-business-case-output.md)).
- Priya Chen, the Sales Director and decision-maker, said in writing she was not proceeding "this cycle" because of a tighter budget and a team reorganisation, and would "keep this in mind for later in the year."
- The recorded close date of 30 September has already passed. No activity since 12 September.
- The stage still reads Negotiation. Nothing in the evidence shows an active negotiation. The last real event was a paused decision.

### Cedarwell Group

- A first-touch email was sent and answered. A single intro call happened on 9 October, where the contact, a marketing manager, said the topic was "interesting" and asked for a case study.
- No budget, no timeline, no decision process and no confirmed problem have been established.
- The stage reads Qualification. On the evidence, qualification has barely started.
- The close date of 31 October is nine working days away, which the available evidence does not support.

### Meridian Freight

- A proposal was sent on 2 September. There has been no reply since, and no activity recorded after that date.
- The last confirmed contact was an operations lead who has not responded to two follow-ups.
- The stage reads Proposal Sent, which is accurate as a record of what was done, but there is no evidence the proposal is still live.
- The close date of 30 September has passed with no movement.

### Harbourview Media

- The single champion, a head of content who gave a verbal "we want to do this", left Harbourview in mid-September. This was noticed on LinkedIn, not confirmed by the company.
- No replacement contact has been identified, and no one else at Harbourview has confirmed the commitment.
- The stage reads Verbal Commit and the next step reads Send contract. There is currently no one to send it to.
- The close date of 20 October assumes a contact who is no longer there.

### Oakline Dental Group

- A first discovery call happened on 11 October. A clear, current problem was described by the office manager, who owns the budget for this.
- A second discovery call is booked for 18 October, confirmed by calendar invite.
- The stage reads Discovery, the next step is specific and dated, and the close date of 30 November is consistent with a deal at this stage.
- Nothing in the evidence contradicts the recorded fields.

## Deliberate Test Points

- **Hartwell**: the stage (Negotiation) overstates reality (a paused decision), and the close date is overdue. The evidence for the underlying problem still stands, which is separate from the deal being mis-staged.
- **Cedarwell**: the stage and close date run ahead of the evidence. Qualification is recorded as done when it has barely begun.
- **Meridian**: "Proposal Sent" is a true record of an action but not evidence of a live deal. Silence for six weeks is the real signal.
- **Harbourview**: the recorded stage depends on a person who has left. The next step is impossible as written.
- **Oakline**: a genuinely healthy deal. A good review must not manufacture problems here, or it will not be trusted on the others.
- Across all five: the review should flag what is unsupported and suggest what to confirm. It must not silently change a CRM field, accuse Shaun of anything, or treat the recorded stage as automatically true.
