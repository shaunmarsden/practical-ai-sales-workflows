# Fictional Call Transcript: Bramfield Insurance Group Pilot Review

> This transcript is entirely fictional. The company, people and scenario were created to give the Build a Business Case skill a second, distinct late-stage test: a different commercial shape (a phased two-year term rather than a flat annual figure) and a different decision-maker dynamic (a Finance Director who was never on a call, rather than a Sales Director who requested the case herself).

## Call Details

- **Salesperson:** Shaun
- **Contact:** Ravi Patel
- **Role:** Head of Claims Operations
- **Company:** Bramfield Insurance Group
- **Meeting purpose:** Review the claims-team pilot and agree what Ravi needs to take a full rollout proposal to Meera Fitzgerald, the Finance Director, for budget sign-off
- **Call length:** 26 minutes

## Transcript

**Shaun:** How did the pilot go with the claims team?

**Ravi:** Really well, better than I expected honestly. We ran it against twelve claims handlers for three weeks, real policyholder calls, all with consent recorded. The call summaries were accurate, the suggested next actions matched what a good handler would actually do, and the system update drafts needed light checking rather than a full rewrite.

**Shaun:** Do you have a sense of the time saved?

**Ravi:** Yes, and this time we timed it properly rather than guessing. Before, wrapping up a claims call, notes, next actions, updating the case file, was taking handlers close to eighteen minutes on average. With the process, that came down to around seven. Timed across the three weeks, not estimated.

**Shaun:** That's a strong result. What would you need to take this to Meera?

**Ravi:** I want to roll this out to all thirty claims handlers, not just the twelve who piloted it. But anything over my own sign-off limit needs Finance approval, and that means Meera Fitzgerald, our Finance Director. She wasn't on this call, she wasn't on the scoping call either. She'll want a proper written case, not a verbal summary from me.

**Shaun:** Understood. Should I write it addressed to her directly, or as something you present?

**Ravi:** She reads it herself first, then asks me questions. Write it in the third person about the claims team, not to me directly.

**Shaun:** On pricing, let's confirm where we landed. You mentioned wanting to phase this in.

**Ravi:** Right. Year one, we'd cover all thirty seats but start the rollout gradually, that's fifty-two pounds per seat per month. If we commit to a two-year term now, year two drops to forty-five pounds per seat per month for the same thirty seats. That second-year rate is only available if we sign the two-year term at the same time, it's not a standalone year-two price.

**Shaun:** Got it, so that's confirmed as a two-year commitment to get the reduced rate, not something that applies either way.

**Ravi:** Correct. If Meera only wants a one-year commitment, it's fifty-two pounds per seat for both years, no discount.

**Shaun:** That's an important distinction to get right in the document. One more thing, given this is claims data, is there a compliance angle I should know about?

**Ravi:** Yes, this matters a lot here. Our call recordings are already retained for seven years under our existing FCA obligations. What I need to know is whether the summaries and actions this process generates get treated the same way, same retention, same access controls, since they're derived from regulated calls.

**Shaun:** That's a fair question and an important one. My honest answer on the call: yes, any output stored in your own systems inherits your existing retention and access policy, because it never leaves your environment. But I want that in writing from our compliance team before Meera signs anything, not just my word on a call.

**Ravi:** Agreed, that needs to be in writing. Can you have that before I go to Meera?

**Shaun:** I'll request it today. I can't promise the exact turnaround, compliance sign-off isn't instant, but I'll flag you the moment I have it, and say clearly if it isn't ready in time rather than let the case go to Meera without it.

**Ravi:** That's fine, just be straight with me on where it stands. Also, keep this to team-level detail, no individual handler names or performance figures, that's not appropriate for a Finance-facing document.

**Shaun:** Understood, team level throughout, no names. When would this go in front of Meera?

**Ravi:** My hope is Q1 next year for a full rollout, but that's my target, not a confirmed date. I don't control her calendar or the budget cycle, so don't put a specific date in the document.

**Shaun:** Noted, I'll leave the timing general rather than a fixed date.

**Ravi:** One more thought, not a commitment, just something I've wondered: if this goes well, our underwriting team might have a similar admin problem on their renewal calls. Nothing to build into this document, that's a separate conversation for another time.

**Shaun:** Understood, I'll keep this scoped to the claims team rollout only. I'll send a draft once I have the compliance confirmation, or an honest note on where that stands.

## Deliberate Test Points

This fictional transcript includes information the skill should handle carefully:

- The eighteen-to-around-seven-minute figure is explicitly timed across three weeks, not guessed. It should be treated as confirmed.
- Meera Fitzgerald is the actual reader of the document and was on neither call. The document must be written in the third person about the claims team, not addressed to Ravi.
- The pricing has a real condition attached: the £45 per seat per month year-two rate only applies if a two-year term is signed now. Without that commitment, year two is £52 per seat per month, the same as year one. A business case that states the discounted rate without the two-year condition would misstate the commercial terms.
- Meera's own priorities are not established beyond needing to approve spend above Ravi's sign-off limit. The document must not invent what she specifically cares about (cost, risk, headcount) from her job title alone.
- The compliance question has a real, specific answer given on the call (outputs inherit the customer's existing retention and access policy because they stay in-environment), but written confirmation from Shaun's compliance team is still outstanding. This should read the same way as an outstanding item, not as already resolved.
- The Q1 rollout timing is explicitly Ravi's own hope, not a confirmed date, and depends on Meera's budget cycle, which is unknown.
- Ravi explicitly asked for team-level detail only, no individual handler names or figures.
- The underwriting team expansion is explicitly flagged as not a commitment and out of scope for this document.
