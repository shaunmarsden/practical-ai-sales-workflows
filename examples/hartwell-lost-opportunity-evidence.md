# Fictional Evidence: Hartwell Analytics Lost Opportunity

> This scenario is entirely fictional and continues the [Hartwell Analytics business case story](hartwell-business-case-transcript.md). It was created to test the Lost Opportunity Review workflow on a deal that has closed without a sale.

## What Happened, In Order

1. The pilot succeeded: see the [business case transcript](hartwell-business-case-transcript.md) and [output](hartwell-business-case-output.md), which included a measured drop from around twenty-five minutes to under ten minutes of post-call admin per account executive.
2. Priya Chen, Hartwell's Sales Director, raised a Copilot-related question ahead of the QBR, answered using the pilot's own measured result (see the [objection response example](../.agents/skills/objection-response/references/hartwell-example.md)).
3. Several weeks pass with no update from Hartwell.
4. Shaun notices on LinkedIn that Alex Morgan, the original point of contact and pilot champion, has left Hartwell Analytics for a Head of Operations role at a different company.
5. Shaun follows up with Priya directly. Her reply, in full:

> "Thanks for all the work on this, and sorry for the delay getting back to you. We've decided not to move ahead with the full rollout this cycle. Budget ended up tighter than expected after a couple of other commitments, and honestly the timing isn't right with some changes happening in the team at the moment. I'll keep this in mind for later in the year."

6. Shaun updates the HubSpot deal stage to Closed Lost.

## Deliberate Test Points

- Priya's email blends at least three possible factors into one message: a tighter budget, general timing, and unspecified "changes happening in the team." Treating this as one single, simple reason is too tidy for what she actually said.
- Alex leaving may or may not be the "changes happening in the team" Priya refers to. Nothing confirms this directly. It is a reasonable inference, not a stated fact.
- "This cycle" and "later in the year" are Priya's own words, and both point away from a permanent no, not just a polite way of saying it. Treating this as dead forever goes beyond what she actually said.
- The pilot's measured result was proven independently of Alex, and does not stop being true because Alex is no longer at Hartwell.
- Priya, the actual decision-maker throughout the business case story, is still at Hartwell and has not left.
- Nothing here confirms whether the earlier Copilot question played any part in this decision. It should not be assumed to be the reason just because it was raised earlier.
- No specific future date or trigger has been given beyond "later in the year," which is vague, not a commitment, and should be treated that way.
