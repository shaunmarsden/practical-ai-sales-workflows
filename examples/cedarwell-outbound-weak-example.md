# Cedarwell Outbound Weak Example

> This is an entirely fictional, deliberately wrong first-touch message, built from the same [fictional Cedarwell signal](cedarwell-outbound-input.md) as the [completed, correct output](cedarwell-outbound-output.md). Nothing here has been sent. It exists to show what the outbound-prospecting skill's guardrails are actually for, by breaking every one of them on purpose.

## The Weak Message

```text
Subject: Unlock Operational Efficiency for Cedarwell Group

Hi Marcus,

Hope this finds you well. My name is Jordan, and I'm a Senior Growth Partner at a company helping logistics businesses like yours unlock ROI through AI-driven, best-in-class operational workflows.

I noticed Cedarwell Group is hiring an Operations Reporting Coordinator, which tells me you're probably already thinking about how to modernise your reporting stack and streamline your ops tech. Companies at your stage are usually already evaluating solutions like ours, and I'd love to get on your radar before the quarter closes.

We've helped businesses just like yours achieve significant efficiency gains through our platform's synergistic, AI-native approach to cross-functional reporting. I only have a couple of slots left this month for a discovery call, so I wanted to reach out directly.

Would you be free for a quick 30-minute call this week or next to explore how we could help?

Best regards,
Jordan
```

## What This Gets Wrong, Point by Point

- **The subject line names the pitch outright** ("Unlock Operational Efficiency"), which is exactly what the skill's subject-line rule warns against. It reads as a marketing send, not an internal message, and a specific, curious reader is less likely to open it than they would a subject like "quick question."
- **"Hope this finds you well" opens the message.** The skill's guardrails call this out directly as invisible filler that a high-volume reader has learned to skip past.
- **Jordan's name, title and company come before anything about Marcus or Cedarwell.** The reader has to get through a full sentence of who-the-sender-is before anything relevant to them appears. The good version never introduces the sender by title at all; it goes straight to the specific problem.
- **"Unlock ROI," "best-in-class," "AI-driven," "synergistic," "AI-native," "cross-functional reporting"** are jargon a reader will skim past without registering, not language grounded in anything Cedarwell actually said. None of it is anchored to the job advert's own wording, which is the one real, verifiable signal available here.
- **"You're probably already thinking about" and "companies at your stage are usually already evaluating solutions like ours" imply Marcus is already interested and already shopping**, when nothing here confirms either. This is exactly the manufactured-interest anti-pattern the skill's guardrails now name directly.
- **"I only have a couple of slots left this month" invents scarcity that is not real.** Nothing about this cold first-touch message is genuinely time-limited; it is a pressure tactic, and the skill's guardrails now forbid it by name.
- **The ask is a 30-minute call, straight away**, not the low-friction reply the skill calls for. Nothing here justifies a meeting-led approach for a prospect who has never heard from this sender before.
- **No commercial claim is actually confirmed.** "Significant efficiency gains" is stated as a general capability, not something demonstrated or evidenced anywhere in this message, and it is exactly the kind of unconfirmed outcome claim the skill's guardrails forbid.

## The Fix

Every one of these is corrected in the [good version](cedarwell-outbound-output.md): a subject line that does not name the mechanism, an opener anchored to the job advert's actual wording, one small low-friction offer instead of a meeting ask, a value line stated in the reader's own terms, and nothing claimed that was not actually confirmed. Reading the two side by side is the point; the good version alone can look like a matter of taste, whereas this one makes clear which parts of the method are actually load-bearing.
