## How to Read This

Each deal shows the **recorded** position (what the CRM says) next to the **evidence-supported** position (what the available notes actually support), then the gap between them and what to confirm. A gap is not an accusation. It is a prompt to check.

## Northstar Analytics

- **Recorded**: Negotiation, £48,000, close date 30 September (passed).
- **Evidence-supported**: paused. Priya confirmed in writing this is not proceeding this cycle, citing budget and a reorganisation, and pointed to "later in the year".
- **Gap**: the stage reads as an active negotiation, which the evidence does not support. The close date is overdue and needs resetting to a realistic point or clearing.
- **Separate and still true**: the pilot's measured result remains valid regardless of the pause, so the underlying case is not lost, only delayed.
- **To confirm and, if Shaun agrees, update**: move the stage to whatever your CRM uses for a paused or nurture state, and reset the close date to a realistic later-in-year point rather than a passed date. This is Shaun's decision, not an automatic change.

## Cedarwell Group

- **Recorded**: Qualification, £30,000, close date 31 October.
- **Evidence-supported**: one answered email and one intro call. Interest expressed, a case study requested. No budget, timeline, decision process or confirmed problem.
- **Gap**: qualification is recorded as reached when it has barely started, and a close date nine working days away is not supported by anything on file.
- **To confirm**: whether a real problem, budget and timeline exist. Until then, the close date is a guess.
- **Suggested next step**: send the requested case study, and use that contact to ask the questions that would actually qualify this, rather than treating it as already qualified.

## Meridian Freight

- **Recorded**: Proposal Sent, £72,000, close date 30 September (passed).
- **Evidence-supported**: a proposal was genuinely sent on 2 September. Nothing has happened since, across two unanswered follow-ups.
- **Gap**: "Proposal Sent" is a true record of an action, but six weeks of silence is the real state, and the close date has passed.
- **To confirm**: whether this deal is still alive at all. One clear either-or message to the operations lead would settle it.
- **Note**: this is not evidence to delete the deal, only to stop treating a sent proposal as forward momentum. If the next message goes unanswered too, an honest lost or nurture state is more accurate than leaving it in Proposal Sent.

## Harbourview Media

- **Recorded**: Verbal Commit, £55,000, close date 20 October, next step "Send contract".
- **Evidence-supported**: blocked. The only champion, who gave the verbal commitment, appears to have left in mid-September, noticed on LinkedIn and not confirmed by the company. No replacement contact exists.
- **Gap**: the recorded stage rests entirely on a person who is no longer there, and the recorded next step, "Send contract", cannot be done, since there is no one to send it to.
- **To confirm**: whether the champion has actually left, and who now owns this at Harbourview. Both are unknown, not assumed.
- **Suggested next step**: confirm the departure and find the new owner before treating this as a live late-stage deal. The verbal commitment does not automatically transfer to whoever replaces them.

## Oakline Dental Group

- **Recorded**: Discovery, £18,000, close date 30 November, next step a second discovery call booked for 18 October.
- **Evidence-supported**: matches. A current problem was described by the budget holder, and a dated, calendar-confirmed next step is in place.
- **Gap**: none found. The recorded fields are supported by the evidence.
- **Note**: flagged here deliberately as healthy. A review that invents a problem on a sound deal is a review that cannot be trusted on the others.

## What Not to Do

- Do not change any CRM field automatically. Every suggestion above is for Shaun to decide and action himself.
- Do not read a mis-staged deal as a mistake by the salesperson. Pipelines drift; that is what the review is for.
- Do not treat the recorded stage as evidence of anything. It is a claim to be checked, not a fact.
- Do not invent a reason, a date or a contact where the evidence only supports an unknown.

## Notes for Human Review

- The only confirmed facts here are the recorded CRM fields and the dated events. Priya's pause is confirmed in writing; the Harbourview departure is an inference from LinkedIn and is labelled as such.
- Two deals (Northstar, Meridian) have close dates that have already passed. Those are the most urgent to correct, because an overdue date quietly distorts any pipeline total built from this data.
