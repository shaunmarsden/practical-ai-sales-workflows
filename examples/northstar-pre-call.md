# Fictional Example: Northstar Analytics

> This example is entirely fictional. The company, contact and scenario were created to demonstrate the workflow.

## Call

- **Contact:** Alex Morgan
- **Role:** Head of Revenue Operations
- **Company:** Northstar Analytics
- **Meeting purpose:** Introductory conversation about improving sales workflow efficiency
- **Good outcome:** Understand whether sales administration is a meaningful problem and agree a deeper workflow mapping session if appropriate

## Confirmed Context

- The calendar invitation describes the meeting as an introductory workflow discussion.
- Alex is listed in the supplied information as Head of Revenue Operations.
- A previous email says the team is reviewing how sales information moves between calls, email and its CRM.

## Assumptions to Test

- Repetitive administration may be taking time away from selling.
- Inconsistent information may be making forecasting or handover harder.

## Relevance Hypothesis

If the team is manually moving information between calls, email and its CRM, an AI assisted process for structuring notes and next actions may be worth exploring.

## Opening

> Alex, thanks for making the time. You mentioned that you're reviewing how information moves between calls, email and the CRM. I thought it would be useful to understand where that process currently creates the most work before we talk about any possible solution.

## Discovery Questions

1. What prompted you to look at the workflow now?
2. Where does the team currently lose the most time or context?
3. What happens after a sales call, and which parts still rely on someone remembering to do them?
4. If you improved one part of the process this quarter, what would have the greatest effect?

## Conversation Paths

### The Problem Is Relevant

- **Explore:** Frequency, people involved, current tools, failure points and business effect
- **Evidence to listen for:** Rework, delayed follow up, missing fields, inconsistent notes or unreliable handovers
- **Sensible next step:** Map one representative workflow using sample or sanitised data

### The Hypothesis Is Wrong or Not a Priority

- **Ask:** Which operational issue is taking priority instead?
- **Alternative area to explore:** Whether preparation, research or pipeline review is creating more friction
- **Sensible close:** Record the actual priority and agree whether another conversation would be useful

### Voicemail or No Answer

> Hi Alex, it's Shaun. We were due to speak about how information currently moves between sales calls, email and your CRM. I'll send a short note so it's easy to rearrange if the conversation is still useful. Thanks.

## Risks and Unknowns

- The scale of the sales team is unknown.
- No specific CRM or AI platform has been confirmed.
- The administrative problem is still a hypothesis and must not be presented as fact.
