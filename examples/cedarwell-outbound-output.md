## Why This Message Is Shaped This Way

- **The opener** quotes the job advert's own wording almost directly, rather than a generic industry observation, so it reads as specific to Cedarwell rather than sent to every logistics company this week.
- **The offer** is a short first look, not a meeting request. It is small enough to actually produce quickly and genuinely useful on its own, which is the low-friction offer the skill calls for.
- **The value line** is stated in the reader's own terms, coordinator time, not a generic productivity claim.
- **The next step** is a reply, not a calendar link. Nothing here justifies skipping straight to a meeting-led ask.
- **The depot expansion is deliberately not mentioned.** It is real context for why Cedarwell might be outgrowing its current process, but it is not evidence of the reporting problem itself, and including it would risk implying more certainty than the advert alone supports.

## Pipeline Decision

Send as a first-touch message, log the target and the send in the CRM or tracker so the same message is never sent twice, and stop the sequence immediately if a reply arrives, positive or otherwise, rather than letting a second scheduled touch go out regardless.
