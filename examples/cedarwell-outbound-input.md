# Fictional Outbound Signal: Cedarwell Group

> This scenario is entirely fictional, separate from the Northstar Analytics story, since outbound prospecting happens before any call exists. Cedarwell Group, Marcus Webb and every detail were created to test the outbound prospecting skill and workflow.

## The Ideal Customer Profile

The company sells the same generic AI-assisted post-call and admin workflow that runs through this repository's other scenarios. A good outbound target is a company of roughly 20 to 200 people with a named, reachable buyer who has plausible authority over an operations or process decision, and a specific, verifiable public signal tied to a real manual-admin problem, not just a company that seems like a plausible fit.

## What Research Turned Up

- **Company:** Cedarwell Group, a 60-person regional logistics company.
- **Signal:** an open job advert for an "Operations Reporting Coordinator," which lists "manually compiling weekly delivery and exception reports across three depot systems" as a core responsibility.
- **Contact:** Marcus Webb, Operations Director, named in a recent local business press piece about the company's depot expansion.
- **CRM check:** no existing record for Cedarwell Group; this would be a new entry, not a duplicate contact.

## Deliberate Test Points

- The job advert is a real, specific, verifiable signal: it names the actual manual, cross-system process. A generic "logistics companies are adopting AI" observation would not be grounded in anything Cedarwell-specific, and using it instead of the advert's own wording would fail the guardrail against a generic hook.
- Marcus Webb's role, Operations Director, makes him a plausible buyer for an operations tooling or process problem. The advertised coordinator role is the person who would feel the pain, not the buyer with authority to fix it.
- The depot expansion is real public information, and can be used as context for why the company might be growing into this kind of problem, but it is not itself proof of a reporting problem. The job advert is the actual evidence; the expansion only explains why now might be a plausible moment.
- Cedarwell's size and the named, reachable contact route make this a reasonably strong target, not a marginal one. Nothing here should be inflated into urgent, must-act-now framing the evidence does not support.
- This is a first-touch message, not a warm follow-up. The first ask should be low-friction, a reply, not a meeting, unless a meeting-led approach has been deliberately chosen for this specific prospect, which nothing here justifies.
