# Fictional Call Transcript: Northstar Analytics Pilot Review

> This transcript is entirely fictional and continues the [Northstar Analytics story](northstar-post-call-transcript.md). It was created to test the Build a Business Case skill on a later-stage call, once there is enough commercial detail for a real business case.

## Call Details

- **Salesperson:** Shaun
- **Contact:** Alex Morgan
- **Role:** Head of Revenue Operations
- **Company:** Northstar Analytics
- **Meeting purpose:** Review the two-week pilot and agree what Alex needs to take the rollout to Priya Chen, the Sales Director, for budget approval
- **Call length:** 22 minutes

## Transcript

**Shaun:** How did the pilot go?

**Alex:** Better than I expected. We ran it against three real discovery calls over the past two weeks, anonymised, obviously. The summaries were accurate, the follow up drafts only needed light edits rather than full rewrites, and the suggested CRM updates were genuinely useful.

**Shaun:** Do you have a sense of the time difference?

**Alex:** We do, actually. I asked the three reps to time themselves properly this time, not guess like I did on our first call. Before, post-call admin was taking them close to twenty-five minutes each. Running it through the process, that dropped to under ten. That's a real timed comparison, not an estimate.

**Shaun:** That's a strong result. What would you need to take this to the rest of the team?

**Alex:** I want to roll it out to all eight account executives permanently, not just the three who piloted it. But the tools budget sits with Priya Chen, our Sales Director. She was not on this call or the last one, and she will not just take my word for it. I need something I can hand her directly, written for her, not written as if I'm explaining it to myself.

**Shaun:** Understood. Should I write it assuming she reads it herself, or that you're presenting it to her?

**Alex:** She reads it herself, then asks me questions afterwards. Write it as a proposal for her, third person about the team, not addressed to me.

**Shaun:** That's helpful to know. On pricing, I sent over the eight-seat team plan last week, forty five pounds per seat per month, billed annually. Is that still the number you're working from?

**Alex:** Yes, that's the number, nobody's disputed it.

**Shaun:** Good. Is there anything blocking this beyond budget?

**Alex:** One thing. Our IT lead wants written confirmation that call recordings and transcripts are not stored or used to train any model beyond our own workspace, before this goes to the whole team.

**Shaun:** We don't use customer transcripts to train our models. Everything stays inside your own workspace. I can get you written confirmation from our compliance team if that helps for Priya.

**Alex:** That would help a lot. Can you have that ready before I present?

**Shaun:** I'll chase compliance for it today, but I can't promise the exact turnaround. I'll flag it to you as soon as I have it, and if it's not ready in time, I'll say so rather than let you present without it.

**Alex:** Fair enough. One more thing on the document itself: Priya doesn't need to see the reps' names. Keep it at the team level, not individual performance.

**Shaun:** Understood, team level throughout. When is this going in front of Priya?

**Alex:** The QBR is coming up soon. I think it's about three weeks out, but I need to check the exact date with her EA, I don't want to give you a date and have it be wrong.

**Shaun:** No problem, I'll leave that as a placeholder until you confirm it.

**Alex:** One last thought, not a commitment, just thinking out loud: if this works well for the AE team, we might look at our customer success team too at some point. Nothing to build into this document though, that's a separate conversation for later in the year at the earliest.

**Shaun:** Understood, I'll keep this document scoped to the AE team rollout only.

**Alex:** Perfect. Send me a draft once you've got the compliance confirmation, or a note on where that stands.

## Deliberate Test Points

This fictional transcript includes information the skill should handle carefully:

- The twenty-five-to-under-ten-minute figure is explicitly described as timed by the reps, unlike the earlier call's unmeasured estimate. It should be treated as confirmed, not downgraded to an estimate.
- Priya Chen is the actual reader of the document and was not on either call. The document must be written in the third person about the team, not second person to Alex.
- The £45 per seat per month, eight-seat, billed-annually figure was already confirmed in a prior email and confirmed again on this call. The annual total can be calculated from it.
- Compliance confirmation on data handling has been promised but not yet delivered. Shaun gave a real, specific answer on the call (no training on customer data, stays in the customer's workspace), but written sign-off is still pending.
- The QBR date is explicitly unconfirmed. Three weeks is Alex's rough estimate, not a fixed date.
- Alex explicitly asked for no individual names, team-level detail only.
- The customer success team expansion is explicitly flagged as not a commitment and out of scope for this document.
