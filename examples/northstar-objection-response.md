# Northstar Objection Response

> This is a worked response created from the [fictional objection](northstar-objection-input.md). Nothing has been sent, and no discount or commitment has been offered or authorised.

## The Objection, Restated

Priya has said the number in the business case is higher than she expected to put in front of the QBR, and that she assumed it would come in lower. She has not disputed the pilot result, the fit, or the value.

## Real Driver

Primarily **other people**, not price, despite the surface wording.

The reasoning: Priya holds the budget authority, asked for the business case herself, and has not questioned whether it works. If this were a straightforward price objection, you would expect doubt about the value or a request for a lower figure. Neither is present. What is present is the phrase "putting in front of the room." Her difficulty is not paying the number; it is defending it to finance and other department heads at the QBR. The right response gives her what she needs to win that room, not a smaller number.

There is a secondary **information** element: she may genuinely want to understand how the figure breaks down, since that is what makes it defensible.

## Acknowledge, Isolate, Reframe, Ask

- **Acknowledge**: a number that is bigger than expected is a fair thing to flag before a QBR, and it is reasonable to want it to hold up in the room.
- **Isolate**: check directly whether the concern is the absolute figure, or presenting and defending it to finance and the other department heads, since the right help differs.
- **Reframe**: move from the total to what the total buys per account executive. The pilot's own measured result, the drop from around twenty-five minutes to under ten minutes of post-call admin per account executive, turns the number from a lump-sum cost into a per-head payback she can defend with her own team's measured data rather than a vendor claim.
- **Ask**: offer a short, forwardable one-page cost justification built from the pilot's numbers that she can put in front of finance, or a quick call to walk through the breakdown before the QBR.

## Draft Reply

> Hi Priya,
>
> Thanks, and that is fair to raise before the QBR. Can I check one thing: is the concern the total figure itself, or having to present and defend it to finance and the other leads in the room? The reason I ask is that the most useful thing I can send back is probably different depending on which it is.
>
> If it is the second, the strongest case is your own pilot data rather than anything from me. Across the pilot, post-call admin dropped from around twenty-five minutes to under ten minutes per account executive. Set against the cost per head, that is a payback you can show the room using numbers your own team generated, not a supplier estimate.
>
> I am happy to put that on a single page you can forward or drop straight into the QBR pack, or to jump on a short call beforehand to walk through the breakdown. Which would help more?
>
> Shaun

## Pipeline Decision

Progress, not disqualification and not a discount. Priya is still the decision-maker, still engaged, and has raised a real and answerable need: help defending a number she has already decided is worth presenting. The next step is a dated follow-up tied to the QBR date.

## Notes for Human Review

- No discount has been offered, because none was asked for and none is authorised. If Shaun decides pricing flexibility is genuinely on the table, that is a separate, human decision, not something this response assumes.
- The one-page justification should be built only from the pilot's actual measured numbers, not rounded up or embellished to look better in the room.
- The follow-up should be dated against the real QBR date, which Shaun knows and this draft does not invent.
