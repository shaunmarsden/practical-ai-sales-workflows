# Fictional Call Transcript: Hartwell Analytics

> This transcript is entirely fictional. The company, people and scenario were created to test the Post Call Follow Up workflow.

## Call Details

- **Salesperson:** Shaun
- **Contact:** Alex Morgan
- **Role:** Head of Revenue Operations
- **Company:** Hartwell Analytics
- **Meeting purpose:** Understand how information moves from sales calls into email and the CRM
- **Call length:** 18 minutes

## Transcript

**Shaun:** Thanks for making the time, Alex. You mentioned that you are reviewing how information moves between calls, email and the CRM. What prompted you to look at it now?

**Alex:** It has been irritating people for a while, but it has become more obvious as the team has grown. We have eight account executives now. After a customer call, everyone has their own way of doing things.

**Shaun:** What normally happens after a call?

**Alex:** The rep writes some notes, sends an email and updates the CRM. At least, that is what should happen. Sometimes the notes stay in a notebook or in a call recording. The follow up email usually goes out, but the CRM update can be a day or two later.

**Shaun:** What problem does that create for you?

**Alex:** Mainly visibility. If I look at an opportunity on the same day, I cannot always tell what happened or what the customer agreed to. It also makes our Monday pipeline meeting harder because we spend time asking people for updates.

**Shaun:** Do you know roughly how much time the team spends on the administration after each call?

**Alex:** Not accurately. I would guess somewhere between fifteen and thirty minutes, but that is just my estimate. We have not measured it.

**Shaun:** Which CRM are you using?

**Alex:** HubSpot. We also record most calls in a meeting tool, although I need to check which package we are on and whether it gives us full transcripts.

**Shaun:** If you could improve one part first, what would be most useful?

**Alex:** A consistent summary and a reliable list of next actions. If that could also produce a sensible first draft of the follow up email, even better. I do not want something sending emails automatically.

**Shaun:** That makes sense. The salesperson should check everything before it is sent or added to HubSpot.

**Alex:** Yes. Accuracy matters more than saving five extra minutes. We sometimes discuss pricing or implementation dates, and I would not want a tool inventing a commitment.

**Shaun:** Would it be useful to test the process with one fictional or sanitised call before involving the wider team?

**Alex:** Yes. I can send you an anonymised transcript from a typical discovery call. I should be able to do that by Thursday afternoon, but I need to confirm internally that the transcript is safe to share.

**Shaun:** Great. I will send you a short outline of the test and the information we would extract. Once you have confirmed the transcript can be shared, we can run the example and review the result together.

**Alex:** That works. Could we hold thirty minutes next Tuesday morning to look at it?

**Shaun:** I think so. I need to check my diary before I confirm a time. I will include some options in my email today.

**Alex:** Perfect.

**Shaun:** Before we finish, is anyone else likely to need to see the result?

**Alex:** Probably our Sales Director, Priya. She does not need to join the first test, but I would share the output with her if it looks useful. I will check whether she wants anything specific included.

**Shaun:** Understood. I will keep the first test focused on the call summary, actions, follow up email and suggested CRM updates.

**Alex:** Sounds good. Thanks, Shaun.

**Shaun:** Thanks, Alex. I will send the outline and some meeting options later today.

## Deliberate Test Points

This fictional transcript includes information the workflow should handle carefully:

- The team has eight account executives.
- HubSpot is the confirmed CRM.
- The current administration time is an estimate, not a measured fact.
- Alex intends to send an anonymised transcript by Thursday afternoon, subject to internal approval.
- Shaun promised to send a test outline and meeting options today.
- A meeting next Tuesday morning was discussed but no time was confirmed.
- Shaun needs to check his diary.
- Alex needs to check the recording package and whether Priya wants anything included.
- No automated email sending was agreed.
- No pricing, purchase or implementation commitment was made.
