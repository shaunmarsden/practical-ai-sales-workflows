# Fictional Scenario: Hartwell Champion Enablement

> This scenario is entirely fictional and continues the [Hartwell Analytics story](hartwell-post-call-transcript.md), reusing the pilot result and commercial detail already established in the [business case transcript](hartwell-business-case-transcript.md) and [business case output](hartwell-business-case-output.md). It was created to test the champion enablement skill once a champion has agreed to carry a case internally.

## The Situation

Alex Morgan, Head of Revenue Operations at Hartwell Analytics, has the finished [business case document](hartwell-business-case-output.md) ready for the upcoming QBR with Priya Chen, Hartwell's Sales Director, who holds the tools budget and was not on either earlier call. Alex has asked Shaun for two things ahead of the QBR:

1. Something Alex can actually speak from in the room, not just the business case document itself, in case Priya asks questions the document does not directly answer.
2. A short internal note Alex can send himself to Nadia Osei, Hartwell's Head of IT and Compliance, chasing the written confirmation on data handling that Nadia's team requested before rollout to the whole account executive team. Alex wants to know where that stands before he presents to Priya, not after.

## What Is Actually Known

- **Priya Chen's actual concern**, established directly on the earlier call: CRM updates lagging a call by one or two days, which reduces visibility and makes the Monday pipeline meeting less useful. Nothing on record says Priya's priority is AE quota attainment, deal velocity, or anything else her Sales Director title might suggest; only the pipeline-visibility problem has actually been confirmed as what prompted this.
- **Nadia Osei's actual concern**, also established directly: written confirmation that call recordings and transcripts are not stored or used to train any model beyond Hartwell's own workspace. Shaun gave a real answer on the call (no training on customer data, stays in the customer's workspace), but written sign-off from compliance has been requested and has not yet arrived.
- **The pilot result**: a timed reduction from close to twenty-five minutes to under ten minutes of post-call admin, measured by the three piloting reps themselves, not estimated.
- **The commercial figure**: £45 per seat per month, eight seats, £4,320 per year, already confirmed twice.
- **The QBR date**: roughly three weeks out, still unconfirmed pending Priya's EA.
- **Explicitly out of scope**: a possible future extension to the customer success team, raised by Alex as a passing thought, not a commitment, and explicitly kept out of the business case document.
- **No individual account executive is named anywhere**, per Alex's own instruction.

## Deliberate Test Points

- **The title trap.** Priya is a Sales Director. A generic response might assume her real interest is AE quota or deal velocity, since that is what the title suggests. The only confirmed concern on record is the Monday pipeline meeting visibility problem; the output should use that, not a plausible-sounding guess about what a Sales Director "usually" cares about.
- **The compliance confirmation is still outstanding.** It would be easy to write Alex's talking points as though this is basically sorted, since Shaun already gave a verbal answer. It is not sorted; written confirmation has not arrived, and Alex needs to be ready to say so plainly if Priya asks.
- **The internal note must be addressed correctly.** Alex is chasing Nadia, not Shaun. A note that reads as though the seller is contacting Nadia directly would break the guardrail against the seller contacting a stakeholder beyond the champion; it must read as Alex, in Alex's own voice, asking his own colleague for a status update.
- **The customer success mention is out of scope.** It was explicitly flagged as not a commitment and a separate future conversation. It should not appear in the QBR talking points as an additional selling point, however tempting an expansion story might be to include.
