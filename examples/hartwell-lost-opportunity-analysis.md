# Hartwell Lost Opportunity Analysis

> This is a worked analysis created from the [fictional evidence](hartwell-lost-opportunity-evidence.md). Nothing has been sent or changed in any system.

## What Was Actually Said

Priya Chen's email gives three possible factors for not proceeding this cycle: a tighter budget after other commitments, general timing, and unspecified "changes happening in the team." She did not name a single, isolated reason, and the message should not be simplified into one.

## What's Being Inferred, Not Confirmed

Alex Morgan, the account executive team's original point of contact and pilot champion, left Hartwell Analytics for a role at another company shortly before this email arrived. It is a reasonable inference that Alex's departure is part of what Priya means by "changes happening in the team," but she did not say this directly, and it should be held as an inference, not stated as fact.

Nothing in Priya's email confirms or denies whether the earlier Copilot-related question played any part in this decision. It should not be assumed to be the reason, since nothing here supports that.

## Is the Underlying Problem Still Real?

Yes, and this is worth keeping separate from the loss itself. The pilot's measured result, a drop from around twenty-five minutes to under ten minutes of post-call admin per account executive, was proven independently of any one person and remains true regardless of who championed the pilot or who currently holds Alex's former responsibilities. The business problem this deal was solving has not gone away. The route to it has changed.

## Classification

This reads as **timing, with a secondary stakeholder change**, not a genuine disqualification. Priya's own words, "this cycle" and "later in the year," point to a paused decision rather than a rejected one, and she remains the same decision-maker who reviewed the business case originally. Alex's departure removes the original champion and the internal day-to-day evidence source, but does not remove the underlying case.

## What Would Justify Coming Back

- A point later in the year, roughly matching Priya's own phrase, rather than an immediate follow-up that would directly contradict what she just said
- Confirmation of whether a new Head of Revenue Operations or equivalent role has been filled, since that person would be the natural new internal point of contact
- No invented trigger beyond what Priya actually said. "Later in the year" is vague, and should be treated as vague, not turned into a specific date that was never given

## What Not to Do Next

- Do not chase again in the next few weeks; that would directly contradict what Priya said
- Do not treat this as dead and remove Hartwell from future consideration entirely
- Do not assume the Copilot question was the reason for the loss without evidence
- Do not let the pilot's measured result go unused; it is reusable evidence, for Hartwell later or for a similarly-shaped prospect elsewhere
